# PennChart (EPIC)

## Gaining Access to PennChart
You will need to complete a series of online trainings before gaining access to PennChart. PennChart (EPIC), is the secure system that the Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania system uses to store information about its patients, their medical histories, test results, scheduling, and communication between healthcare providers. You will need to use PennChart primarily to check patients’ medical and MRI histories to determine whether they are good matches for the study.

## Accessing PennChart
First, go to the [Remote Access Portal](https://pennmedaccess.uphs.upenn.edu/my.policy) and log in with your UPHS account. Select PennChart & CitrixApps and log in with your UPHS account again. Then, select PennChart and open it up after it has finished downloading. The PennChart app will pop up, and then you will log in again with your UPHS account. Make sure you are in “HUP PSYCHIATRY MD VIRTUAL”.

## Physician Schedules
One of the ways to recruit participants is through screening patient charts. To do this, you must create a schedule that groups all of the physicians schedules together. Once in PennChart, go to the `Schedule` tab, found in the top left corner (you can also get there by Ctrl+Shift+2). On the left side, press the `Create` button with the green plus sign next to it. Choose a name for the schedule, such as “MS Providers”. Then, click on the `Configuration` tab, and search by `Provider [All Depts]`. Add the following providers to your list:

## Screening Patients in PennChart
There are a few ways in which you will arrive at the screening stage: 

1. A provider may refer a patient to you. You can see who they referred to you by navigating to the `In Basket` tab, and using the `Staff Msg` and `CC’d Charts` tabs to find messages sent to you. Click on the message, and then click their hyperlinked name to view their chart.
2. You may screen a providers schedule. In the `Schedule` tab, select a provider from your list (that you created above), and search through their patients. By clicking once on a patient, you will get a preview of their information. If it seems as though they might be eligible, press `Review` at the top middle of your screen to open the patients’ chart.
3. A patient expresses interest to you via in-person, phone, or email. Sometimes the patient will reach out directly to you. In that case, you can navigate to the chart by clicking on the `Chart Review` button in the bar at the top of the page. You can search for the patient using either their MRN or by typing in their first and last name. 

*Once you have opened their chart, follow these steps to verify eligibility:*

The `Demographics` tab in their chart will provide basic information required to register patients, including their sex, date of birth, address, and contact information. MS diagnosis can be confirmed by visiting the `Problem List` tab, but the most useful information can be gleaned from the notes and the radiology reports in the Encounters and Imaging tabs. Under `Encounters` you can view past doctor’s appointments within the MS center and see who the patient’s provider is. If you need additional information about the visit or want to gauge the current state of the patient’s disorder, you can click on a specific office or virtual visit with a provider and read the notes. You will need to log the patient’s most recent clinical MRI scan date as well as the location where their clinical scans are done. You will need to find the imaging physician order to determine where the scans were collected, which can be identified by clicking on the scan results in the imaging or in the results tab and scrolling down to the original order. Make sure to fill out all necessary information in the Call Log from their charts. It is a good idea to record their basic information (full name, DOB, age, email, phone number, address, and medications [name and dosage], found in the `Medications` tab) on a sticky note before your visit to have easy access to this information.
