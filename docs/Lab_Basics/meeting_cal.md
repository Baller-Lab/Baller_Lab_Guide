# Lab Meetings

The Baller Lab meets weekly for lab meetings for ~1 hour in Dr. Baller’s office. Here, we discuss a variety of topics, with some examples listed below. It is important that prior to every lab meeting, you prepare an agenda to discuss with Dr. Baller about some, or all, of the topics listed below:

- Any questions relating to the study
- Any updates or changes relating to the study
- A brief summary of what you did the week before, and what your goals are for the upcoming week
- Current or future science projects
- Goals for the following month to year
- Upcoming deadlines and planning on how to reach those goals
- Anything else you would like to discuss!

# Google Calendar

The Baller Lab uses a shared Google Calendar to for the following:

- Study visits
    - Dr. Baller with list her availability to be on call for safety assessments, and it is during these periods in which you are able to schedule visits.
    - When you schedule a participant, delete the standing “available for visits” placeholder, and put in your study visit information (block off 3 hours).
    - These should be named “[BBLID] - [virtual/[in-person]”
        - If it is in-person, include the name of the assessment room in the title
    - If it is virtual, include the Zoom link in the description.
- Lab meeting
    - Your standing lab meeting should be included and updated if it is ever moved to a different time or date.
- Feedback sessions
    - Dr. Baller will have her 3 standing Tuesday morning feedback sessions in the calendar, named “avail for CNB review (#)”. If you have schedule a feedback session, delete the placeholder event, and add the feedback session event, with the label “Feedback Session - [BBLID]”. 
- PTO/WFH
    - Any PTO should be included in the calendar. Furthermore, if there are certain days that you are planning on working from home, it is also helpful to note this.
- Dr. Baller’s schedule
    - If Dr. Baller is teaching/busy/etc., she may include this in the calendar as well.
- Any additional meetings/events
    - Meetings such as Coordinators, standing meetings with other labs, Ted’s lab meeting, etc. can also be included.
